## What is BlueSAT?

BlueSAT is UNSW's project-based space society. Unlike a lot of university clubs, it's structured around real engineering projects with tangible deliverables. The three main projects are:

- **Off-World Robotics (OWR)** — rover development
- **High Altitude Balloon** — atmospheric payloads
- **Groundstation** — satellite communication infrastructure

I joined as part of the groundstation team and eventually became lead.

## Groundstation v0

### The brief

v0 was about getting something working. With a team of just **4 people**, the goal was to build a functional groundstation capable of tracking and communicating with satellites in low Earth orbit.

We based the design on the **SatNOGS rotator v3** — an open-source antenna rotator developed by the Libre Space Foundation. Taking an existing open-source design and actually getting it operational is harder than it sounds: sourcing parts, understanding the control systems, integrating the RF hardware, and debugging a system that you didn't build from scratch.

![Groundstation v0](/Portfolio/assets/images/bluesat-v0.jpg)

We got it working. For a 4-person team with limited resources, that felt like a genuine win.

## Groundstation v1

v1 is a different challenge entirely — a **clean-sheet design** built around our own requirements rather than adapting someone else's. More on this soon.

## What leading a small technical team taught me

Taking on a lead role on v0 with only 4 people meant there was no hiding. Everyone had to carry real responsibility, and as lead I had to make decisions with incomplete information and keep momentum going when things stalled.

The technical side was demanding, but the organisational side — keeping a small volunteer team aligned and motivated around a long-horizon project — was its own kind of engineering problem.
